CAREER FRAMEWORK

There are five distinct Career Streams.

Career Streams represent the career types within the organization, characterized by unique responsibilities.

Career Stream
Description

Support

(S)

Individual contributors that provide organizational related support or service (administrative or clerical) or roles operating in a “hands on” environment (production assemblers and techs) in support of daily business and operational activities. Work typically requires a high school degree or 1-2 year vocational training / associates degree.

Professional

(P)

Individual contributors with responsibility in a professional discipline or specialty, and may oversee a few direct reports. The majority of time is spent overseeing the design, implementation or delivery of processes, programs and policies using specialized knowledge and skills typically acquired through advanced education (typically University).

Technical

(T)

Individual contributors with responsibility in a technical discipline or specialty. The majority of time is spent developing solutions and performing the design, implementation or delivery of products, services, systems and solutions using specialized knowledge and skills (technical principles, theories and concepts) normally acquired through specialized training and/or advanced education (typically University).

Management

(M)

People management and supervisory professionals focusing on tactical, operational activities within a specified area. Levels within the management career stream have direct reports. The majority of time is spent overseeing areas of responsibility, planning, prioritizing and/or directing the responsibilities of employees. Goal achievement is typically accomplished through performance of direct/indirect reports.

Executive

(E)

Includes US Organization Heads, Function Heads, and Sub-Function Heads providing strategic vision and/or tactical/strategic direction across the US organization. The majority of time is spent overseeing area of responsibility directing the responsibilities of employees. Goal achievement is typically accomplished through performance of direct and/or indirect reports.

CAREER STREAM FAQs

Can I move from one Career Stream to another?

Our career streams align with specific job families and sub-families. If an employee's role is incorrect or they've transitioned within QinetiQ, their job family/sub-family and stream can be adjusted accordingly due to mapping errors or new job responsibilities. Employees should discuss changes with their manager and/or business partner.

To explore new career paths and transition to another stream, employees can consult their manager, HRBP, and InsideFirst Career Coaches for guidance on role requirements and internal opportunities.

For more information on streams alignments, employees should refer to the Families and Sub-Families tab on the Career Level Competencies page.

There are 13 Career Levels.

Career Levels are the hierarchical position of a job within a career stream. Levels recognizes incremental changes in job scope and responsibilities and are consistent across job families. Climb from one Career Level to the next as the needs of the business expand and you consistently demonstrate proficiency at the next level.

CAREER LEVEL FAQs

How was my level determined?

BU leaders, managers, HRBPs, and the ITM and Total Rewards teams evaluated each employee’s roles and responsibilities against newly established, standardized leveling competencies, resulting in updated Job Family, Sub-Family, Stream, and Level for each employee. You can access the leveling competencies for each Level by visiting the Career Level Competencies page.

What if I don't agree with my current level?

We encourage open dialogue, so if you have concerns about your level within the career framework, we encourage you to bring them up to your manager or your HRBP.

How and when can I get promoted to the next Level?

If you're interested in earning a promotion and progressing to the next level, we recommend a review of your current level competencies and assessing the level above using our Career Level Competencies page. Then talk with your manager so they can explain the job attributes associated with the next career level up and help you set personal goals to gain the skills and experience you need.

When the Self-Assessment phase of the Performance Development Review (PDR) process begins in March, it's important to prepare detailed justifications highlighting how you meet the criteria for advancement to the next career level and how you've achieved your performance objectives. If your manager agrees with your assessment and you receive endorsement from your Business Unit (BU) leadership, your application will be submitted for promotion review in May.

All promotion applications are carefully evaluated by the promotion review panel May-June. If you successfully fulfill all requirements, your manager will notify you during your performance discussion in June, and you will officially be promoted in July.

If I change Career Streams, Job Families, or Sub-Families, does that change my Career Level?

Depending on the specific role, your skills, experience, and alignment to the leveling competencies, changing career paths, job families, and/or sub-families may change your career level. To explore what a change would mean for you, we encourage you to consult your manager and HRBP.

Why do the levels start at six?

Your job levels start at level 6 to align with QinetiQ Group's leveling scheme, which consists of a total of 18 levels. However, within our organization, we have condensed these levels to 12. By starting at level 6, we are able to seamlessly integrate with Group's framework and effectively match the 18 levels.

Career Champions are part coach, part mentor, part communicator, part cultural ambassador, and yes, part leader.

Research shows that 70% of employees feel they do not get enough time to discuss their goals and career aspirations with their leaders. That is not unique to QinetiQ; that is an industry wide challenge. The Career Champion role is critical to truly taking care of our employees and ensuring we are creating and cultivating a people-first culture. According to Forbes, "Career Champions tend to be more influential in motivating employees to do their best work and feel engaged."

How exactly will the People care for employees?

  • Career Champions are career coaches that provide leadership, career guidance, and act as liaisons for HR/ITM programs, resources and benefits.
  • Career Champions are advocates for their assigned employees, getting to know them + their professional goals and aspirations; help new employees transition from “Day 1” onboarding to engaged QinetiQ teammates.
  • Career Champions monitor and encourage their people’s development and assess their competencies, providing periodic feedback and writing mid-year and annual reviews (with performance input from managers).
  • Career Champions motivate, coach, and counsel; they help build trust, resiliency, and professional growth; they have a role in conflict resolution, change management, and more!
  • Career Champions are culture ambassadors, helping employees understand QinetiQ’s core values and principles, facilitating employee engagement.

In a fast-growing and busy company like QinetiQ, we need to carve out the resources required to make this happen. In this case, the people resources. We have amazing managers doing truly impressive things for our clients and our company. But some of them just don't have the bandwidth to focus on individual careers and the professional growth of every one of their people. Career Champions will be dedicated to this effort; we ensure that each Career Champion has a maximum of four people they support. Listening to those goals and aspirations will be their number one priority. The research is clear: employees who feel like their voice is being heard are more likely to stay and be more productive.

New employees will be prompted to Opt In or Out of the Career Champion program within 60 days of hire. Should you Opt-In, your Career Champion should continue coaching you throughout your QinetiQ career journey. As employees move from program to program over time, they will maintain their same Career Champion. This will eventually create a people network across programs and even divisions.

INSIDEFIRST FAQs

Who is eligible?

Any employee meeting performance expectations, as assigned in their current Career Level, is eligible for InsideFirst. We encourage one year of tenure on a project at QinetiQ in order to gain the skills and experience offered by the project and provide consistency in coverage for our clients, but will consider eligibility sooner under certain circumstances.

How do I get started?

To get started, visit the InsideFirst Career Center.

How long does it take to transfer to another project or role?

The time to transfer varies depending on several factors and involves coordination among multiple parties. In order to transfer, an appropriate role must first be identified, where an employee matches the skillsets and meets the job requirements. Once a funded, open position is identified, a transition plan will be developed and a transfer can be coordinated.

Will my salary and Career Level change if I transfer?

The intent of the InsideFirst program is to afford employees with opportunities for professional development and growth that align with their career goals. QinetiQ offers a well-defined promotion process to address salary increases and Career Level promotions. Transfers to a position at a higher Career Level allow employees to demonstrate performance in a new role and would result in promotability within the year-end promotion cycle. We expect most salary increases to occur in July after Performance Development Review (PDR).

What is the role of the Career Coach?

The Career Coach is a resource for QinetiQ employees when participating in the InsideFirst program and serves as the liaison between the employee, managers, support staff, and our Executive Leadership. The Career Coach is available to discuss your interests and career goals, the ITM program, and the process for InsideFirst. Connect with the Career Coach at CareerCoach@us.qinetiq.com.

Accounting
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing or performing work in general financial accounting activities of the organization. Activities include financial transaction recording standards; control/reconciliation of accounts and records; cost accounting/budgeting; accounting reports/schedules for internal audiences (management reporting) and for external audiences (compliance reporting); may include administrative/transactional accounting support work, such as ledger maintenance and data entry.
Finance
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for preparing and maintaining records of all transactions (e.g. deposits, purchase orders, invoices, refunds). Activities include processing reimbursements; preparing and reconciling ledgers and budgets; preparing monthly and quarterly financial reports; preparing financial paperwork; supporting the financial operations of the organization; and administering and reviewing financial plans and budgets.
Financial Planning & Analysis
Positions in this sub-family focus on short and long-term financial planning and/or financial analysis of business operations for the purpose of measuring and forecasting future business performance. Activities include analysis of internal financial information (e.g., profit loss accounts, financial statements, working capital, costs, prices, expenses, revenues, rates of return, etc.); analysis of external economic conditions and their impact on business operations (e.g., inflation, interest rates, exchange rates, etc.); and financial modeling to measure and forecast potential impacts of financial transactions and corporate development activities.
Human Capital (P)
This job family supports the management of the organization's human resources. Activities include developing human resource management strategies and policies to meet business needs; planning, administering and reviewing activities concerned with recruitment, training and development, compensation and benefits, mobility, talent acquisition, diversity, talent management and organization development and employee/labor relations.
Human Resources
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing or performing work across multiple Human Resources sub-families including: general HR program/policy development, administration, and compliance; general business support; employee hiring, onboarding, termination, and records maintenance; employee and labor relations and communications; rewards program coordination and/or administration; relocation services (domestic and international); immigration services; HR related training. In some organizations, may also be accountable for HR Business Partnership (consulting and coaching), Mobility (strategy and design), Staffing & Recruiting, Talent & Organization Performance, Training & Development, and Human Resource Information Systems (HRIS).
Talent Acquisition
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for attracting, finding and acquiring skilled labor for organization needs. Activities include talent evaluation and screening (assessing skills, testing, ranking, reference checking, etc.); job advertising and posting internally and externally, including third party recruiting agencies; talent prospecting at employment fairs and on campus at educational institutions; talent staffing and planning; managing new employee induction process; may be responsible for exit interviews
Talent Management
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for designing, implementing, and administering programs that make the best use of an organization's human capital and ensures maximum return from their talent. Activities include conducting talent assessments; organizational design analysis; planning for leadership and management succession; managing performance measurement systems; conducting employee/engagement research; and workforce planning & analytics.
Total Rewards
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for compensation and benefits programs that align rewards with organization goals. Activities include analyzing and modeling merit and other salary increases; managing job evaluation systems; analyzing market data to assess the organization's competitive position; managing incentive programs; managing insurance, benefit, superannuation, and retirement programs; and selecting and managing compensation & benefits consultants.
Information Technology (T)
This job family manages or performs work associated with analysis, design, implementation, operation, deployment, and support of the organization's information technology resources (including computer hardware, operating systems, communications, software applications, data processing and security), telecommunication systems, and software/database products by internal staff, outsourcing staff, or consultants. Activities include developing information technology strategies, polices and plans; maintenance and use of information technology resources; training and supporting technology users; telecommunications network planning, operations and site acquisition; developing PC, online, and mobile games; and internet product management and operations.
Cyber Security
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing or performing work associated with developing, communicating, implementing, enforcing and monitoring security controls to protect the organization's technology assets from intentional or inadvertent modification, disclosure or destruction. Activities include designing, testing, and implementing secure operating systems, networks, and databases; password auditing, network based and Web application based vulnerability scanning, virus management and intrusion detection; conducting risk audits and assessments, providing recommendations for application design; monitoring and analyzing system access logs; and planning for security backup and system disaster recovery.
IT Business Systems Analysts
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing or performing work associated with IT business analysis. Activities include identifying and analyzing business needs; conducting requirements gathering; defining scope and objectives; making recommendations for solutions or improvements to business processes that can be accomplished through new technology or alternative uses of existing technology; and translating business requirements into application requirements.
IT Infrastructure & Systems Administration
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing or performing work associated with IT infrastructure & systems administration (internal or outsourcing staff) including installation, configuration, administration, support and maintenance of IT software and hardware systems, IT data/voice network systems, and internal databases. Activities include ensuring the uptime, performance, and resource availability of the computers and other IT systems; matching the organization's resources with the demand placed on them; monitoring organization's wide area and/or local area network performance; identifying and resolving network problems; upgrading network hardware, software and telecommunications equipment; IT data center facility operations; and ensuring the quality, accuracy and security of databases.
IT User Support
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for providing support to employee end users in areas of personal computers/servers/mainframe applications, data/voice network, and ERP systems. Activities include acquiring, installing, and upgrading PC components & software and planning for/responding to service outages; diagnosing problem source through discussions with users and coordinating with internal organization support and operations groups and/or with vendors to resolve problems; responding to user requests to research complex problems associated with the organization's telecommunications networks (voice and/or data); and providing real-time end user ERP systems support, problem identification, and training to facilitate knowledge transfer and prevent problem reoccurrence and knowledge transfer.
Network Engineering
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for designing, implementing, and maintaining robust and secure network infrastructures, leveraging their technical expertise to optimize network performance, reliability, and scalability. Activities include: designing and configuring network infrastructure components (e.g. routers, switches); performing network capacity planning and optimization; troubleshooting network issues and implementing solutions; conducting security assessments and implementing network security measures; managing network documentation and configuration standards.
Intelligence (P)
This job family runs the intelligence activities of the organization. Activities include supporting plans; conducting research; preparing, integrating, and publishing intelligence studies, papers, and briefings; conducting analysis supporting intelligence; testing, validating, and implementing algorithms; and maintaining government furnished databases and software applications.
Counterintelligence
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for identifying, assessing, and mitigating threats to organizational security and sensitive information through intelligence gathering, analysis, and countermeasures. Activities include: developing countermeasures and security protocols to mitigate risks; conducting threat assessments and intelligence analysis; monitoring and analyzing suspicious activities and behaviors; conducting investigations into security breaches or insider threats providing intelligence briefings and threat assessments to stakeholders.
Human Intelligence
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for collecting intelligence information from humans. Activities include screening documents; overseeing interviews and interrogations; conducting screenings; working with translators to interpret information; exploiting information; preparing intelligence reports; and engaging in debriefings.
Intelligence Analysis
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for analyzing intelligence collected to identify threats. Activities include developing assessments using available data/information; facilitating information sharing; applying knowledge to identify and combat threats; handling sensitive information; preparing intelligence reports; and examining information from a variety of resources.
Linguistics
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for accurately transcribing a language and making sense of language patterns. Activities include analyzing documents and audio to translate information; studying languages and cultures; and identifying and summarizing foreign languages.
Technical Exploitation/Operations (T)
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for conducting in-depth technical analysis and operations to exploit vulnerabilities, gather intelligence, and support mission-critical activities, utilizing advanced tools and methodologies. Activities include: conducting digital forensics and malware analysis; exploiting technical vulnerabilities for intelligence collection; developing and deploying technical surveillance tools and methods; supporting offensive cyber operations and network exploitation; conducting research and development of new exploitation techniques.
Legal & Compliance (P)
This job family ensures the organization complies with the legal and regulatory requirements of the country(-ies) in which it operates. Activities include providing legal counsel/support in areas related to the business, including corporate law, real estate, tax, business litigation, transactions/antitrust, compliance, intellectual property, employment/labor, corporate governance, ethics, etc. Regulatory activities ensure the organization's products and business activities are conducted ethically and in compliance with relevant regulations, laws and standards. Audit activities include the examination, verification, evaluation, and reporting on financial, operational, and managerial processes, systems and outcomes to ensure financial and operational integrity and compliance.
Auditing
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for examining, evaluating, and reporting on financial, operational and managerial processes, systems and outcomes to ensure integrity and compliance. Activities include process/operations monitoring and review; financial transaction monitoring and review; systems auditing (e.g., IT systems audit; information security evaluation); and audit reporting for external auditors, regulatory authorities, joint venture partners, etc.
Ethics & Compliance
Positions in this sub-family manage or perform work related to developing and administering organization-specific ethical standards applicable to all employees. Activities include documenting policies and procedures related to the ethical standards established by the business (e.g., policies & procedures related to conflicts-of-interest, confidentiality/privacy, fraud, appropriate use of company assets, interpersonal conduct in the workplace, etc.); developing and conducting employee ethics training and ensure employee attendance/completion of all required courses; auditing compliance with ethics programs & procedures; investigating complaints; and verifying that deficiencies are corrected.
Global Trade Compliance
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for the import/export and transshipment of materials, equipment, products, packages and documents including fulfillment of customs requirements. Activities include managing and planning import process, customs documentation, shipping, and receiving of foreign merchandise to ensure correct arrival time and ensuring compliance with the home and foreign country government laws and customs regulations.
Legal Counsel
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for providing expert advice and guidance on legal matters, ensuring compliance with laws and regulations, managing legal risks, and supporting business objectives through effective legal strategies and counsel. Activities include: drafting and reviewing legal documents (e.g. contracts, agreements); providing legal active and counsel on contractual matters; conducting legal research and analysis on regulatory issues; representing the organization in legal proceedings or negotiations; developing and delivering legal training programs.
Marketing & Communication (P)
This job family runs the communications and marketing activities of the organization. Activities include maintaining positive relations with the public, shareholders, investors, government and other regulatory bodies; representing the company as a point of contact between the organization, local officials, and community leaders; identifying, collecting, and sharing business information and knowledge as well as employee knowledge; change management; representing the organization to promote and sell its products and services to individuals or other organizations; supporting business growth by disseminating information that promotes a favorable view of the organization and its products and services; developing and implementing advertising and publicity campaigns.
Marketing
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for advancing business growth by disseminating information that promotes a favorable view of the organization and its products and services. Activities include developing and implementing advertising/promotional campaigns; conducting market research and identifying and predicting current and future consumer trends; product management, including P&L activity and advertising R.O.I. (Return on Investment) analysis; product planning & development; and promotional materials creation and distribution.
Media Specialists
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for creating and managing multimedia content across various platforms, including digital, print, and social media, to effectively communicate the organization’s message and enhance its brand presence. Activities include: creating multimedia content (e.g. videos, graphics); analyzing media metrics and performing data; collaborating with marketing and communications specialists on campaign strategies; pitching stories and securing media coverage for internal and external campaigns.
Public Affairs
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing external communications, building relationships with key stakeholders, and shaping the organization’s public image through strategic communication initiatives and engagement efforts. Activities include: developing and executing public relations campaigns and initiatives; building relationships with media outlets, influencers, and community stakeholders; drafting press releases, speeches, and other communications materials; organizing and coordinating public events, conferences, and speaking engagements; monitoring and managing the organization’s reputation and public perception.
Strategic Communications
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for developing and delivering the organization's internal communication strategy so that employees can rapidly identify, send, receive, and understand information. Activities include developing, publishing and distributing a range of organization-wide and HR-related materials in traditional print form, electronically or through online technologies, e.g., newsletters, brochures, handbooks, conference and seminar materials, benefit and compensation communications, etc.; ensuring the organization's employees have access to business information and knowledge; and developing, managing and implementing strategies, systems and processes to identify, collect and share employee knowledge.
Production Operation (T)
This job family is responsible for production activities in a manufacturing or field site environment to optimize resource use, minimize costs and maintain quality standards. Activities include planning, managing and reviewing production operations to achieve output and quality objectives; undertaking production activities to transform tangible inputs (e.g., raw materials or semi-finished goods) or intangible inputs (e.g., ideas, information, know how) into finished products or services; developing and implementing production schedules to ensure the effective use of labor, tools, plant and equipment; operating, monitoring and maintaining machines and production equipment; and skilled trades and lower level apprentice craft work.
Manufacturing Production, Processing & Assembly
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing or performing work associated with converting raw materials and components into finished goods. Activities include general production manufacturing work; assembling finished components or parts into finished products; process manufacturing batches of bulk materials/ingredients including mechanical processing (e.g., mixing, grinding, separating, filtering, etc.) and chemical processing (altering the chemical properties of materials through chemical reactions and changing temperature, pressure, etc.); setting up and operating machine tools to convert materials into parts, sub-assemblies, and assemblies; surface finishing of parts, components, and assembled items including washing/painting/plating/anti-corrosion coating, hardening, polishing/engraving/etching, etc.; and packaging materials or finished goods into containers for the purpose of protection, display, and handling.
Repair & Maintenance
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing or performing skilled trades and lower level apprentice craft work for construction, maintenance, and repair of facilities systems, buildings, and equipment. Activities include installation, repair and maintenance in a manufacturing or field site environment; installation, repair, maintenance, dismantle, and movement of large and/or complex industrial equipment; service and repair of automobiles and their parts; and installation, repair and maintenance of industry-specific equipment such as Oil & equipment or equipment utilized in provision of transportation.
Site Operations (P)
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for overseeing the day-to-day operations of physical sites or facilities, ensuring smooth and efficient functioning, optimizing resource utilization, and maintaining a safe and productive work environment. Activities include: overseeing facility maintenance and repair activities; managing inventory and supplies for site operations; ensuring compliance with health, safety, and environmental regulations; supervising site security and access control measures; coordinating logistics for site events and activities.
Project/Program Management (P)
This job family includes the management of projects and programs related to general business requirements, specialized/technical areas, or particular industries. Activities include planning, organizing, and controlling resources/processes to achieve project objectives within scope, time, quality, and budget constraints. Roles include those responsible for general project/program management, in which work requires only general knowledge of the project/business requirements, and no specific technical knowledge. Also includes roles responsible for technical project/program management or particular industries, in which the incumbent has some foundational knowledge in the specific area to advise on scope changes, QA and other more technical aspects of the project.
Acquisitions Management
Positions in this sub-family focus on strategic transaction planning and execution (mergers, acquisitions, partnerships, joint ventures, etc.). This may include company and industry research and analysis; financial evaluation and modelling of potential investment opportunities (comparable analysis, precedent transaction analysis, discounted cash flow analysis, financial impact analysis, etc.); Mergers & Acquisition transaction execution (e.g., strategy, due diligence, negotiation, definitive documentation, and integration).
Learning & Development
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for employee career development to make the best use of employees' current and future abilities. Activities include planning, organizing, and developing training curricula, materials, and programs; analyzing training needs and developing programs to address them; evaluating third part training providers; and managing delivery of internal and externally provided training.
Project/Program Management
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for planning, organizing, and controlling resources/processes to achieve project/program objectives within scope, time, quality, and budget constraints. General project management work requires only general knowledge of the project/business requirements and no specific technical knowledge is required.
Technical Project/Program Management (T)
Positions in this sub-family provide technical support for project engineering activities. Activities include supporting preparation of design, project controls and specifications, schedules, production, transportation, installation, testing and/or commissioning of equipment; reviewing and verifying progress for invoicing or payment as appropriate.
Technical Writing
Positions in this sub-family focus on writing documentation for technical products, systems, processes, equipment operations & maintenance, research findings, etc. for internal or external audiences. Activities include determining an effective structure and layout for complex manuals and other technical documentation; conducting research and liaising with technical staff to ensure accurate interpretation of complex technical data, drawings, and specification; ensuring that appropriate technical content and terminology review occurs and that provision is made for the incorporation of updates and revisions; and editing content to improve clarity, impact, flow, meaning, and readability.
Supply Chain (P)
This job family is responsible for all of the steps involved in getting products from suppliers to customers. Activities include work associated with obtaining goods and services for all types of business processes; finished goods demand inventory planning to achieve targeted quality, wastage, and inventory levels; movement, storage, and inventory control of materials, finished goods, tools, and packaging materials; import/export & customs management.
Delivery Assurance
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing and directing the activities of the distribution center, involving the reception, delivery and control of merchandise. Activities include developing and monitoring budgets; maintaining safety/security and inventory control programs; ensuring distribution center facilities and equipment are properly maintained; proposing improvements in order to optimize the distribution process; exercising general supervision of overall distribution activity, including inventory control, order processing, warehouse activities, transportation, maintenance, and customer service.
Logistics
Responsible for planning, controlling, and implementing processes related to materials and finished goods storage and movement including: Materials Movement & Storage; Finished Goods Movement & Storage; General Logistics Optimization, and Planning & Control.
Procurement
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for work associated with obtaining goods and services for all types of business processes applicable to the organization including Indirect Operations (e.g., Office Supplies, Computers, Travel, Maintenance, Machine Parts, etc.); Direct Operations (e.g., Raw Materials and Services for Manufacturing, Production or Construction; Finished Goods for Retail, etc.). Activities include product/service sourcing, supplier selection, pricing/terms negotiation, order processing, contract administration, and supplier performance management.
Production Planning & Control
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing production to optimize the use of resources and meet production schedules and standards. Activities include developing and implementing production schedules to ensure the effective use of labor, tools, plant and equipment and to coordinate production output with inventory requirements; estimating resource requirements; analyzing production and operational data; and matching the organization's resources with demand to maximize output and minimize production costs.

JOB FAMILIES FAQs

Are some job families delivery-focused and others corporate-focused?

Job Families do not distinguish between delivery-focused positions and corporate-facing ones, so each Job Family can include both delivery-facing and corporate facing employees. For example, an employee supporting QinetiQ US' internal corporate communication efforts and an employee providing communications support to a Department of Transportation contract would both be placed in the Marketing & Communications Job Family.
