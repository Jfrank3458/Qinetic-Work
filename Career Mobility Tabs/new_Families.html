We have identified 16 job families and 78 sub-families within our Career Framework.

The Job Families and their descriptions are listed below. Click on a Job Family to display the Sub-Families that fall within it, as well as their descriptions. (P) and (T) designates whether the Family and its Sub-Families are in the Professional (P) or Technical (T) Career Stream. Executive (E), Management (M), and Support (S), are not indicated, as these streams could apply to any of the Families or Sub-Families.

The (P) or (T) designation next to the Family name applies to the Family and its Sub-Family unless indicated otherwise next to the Sub-Family name. In some cases, a Sub-Family will be in a different Career Stream than its Family.

Data Science
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for creating data mining architectures/models/protocols, statistical reporting, and data analysis methodologies to identify trends in large data sets. Technical Project/Program Management (T)
Positions in this sub-family provide technical support for project engineering activities. Activities include supporting preparation of design, project controls and specifications, schedules, production, transportation, installation, testing and/or commissioning of equipment; reviewing and verifying progress for invoicing or payment as appropriate.
Technical Writing
Positions in this sub-family focus on writing documentation for technical products, systems, processes, equipment operations & maintenance, research findings, etc. for internal or external audiences. Activities include determining an effective structure and layout for complex manuals and other technical documentation; conducting research and liaising with technical staff to ensure accurate interpretation of complex technical data, drawings, and specification; ensuring that appropriate technical content and terminology review occurs and that provision is made for the incorporation of updates and revisions; and editing content to improve clarity, impact, flow, meaning, and readability.
Supply Chain (P)
This job family is responsible for all of the steps involved in getting products from suppliers to customers. Activities include work associated with obtaining goods and services for all types of business processes; finished goods demand inventory planning to achieve targeted quality, wastage, and inventory levels; movement, storage, and inventory control of materials, finished goods, tools, and packaging materials; import/export & customs management.
Delivery Assurance
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing and directing the activities of the distribution center, involving the reception, delivery and control of merchandise. Activities include developing and monitoring budgets; maintaining safety/security and inventory control programs; ensuring distribution center facilities and equipment are properly maintained; proposing improvements in order to optimize the distribution process; exercising general supervision of overall distribution activity, including inventory control, order processing, warehouse activities, transportation, maintenance, and customer service.
Logistics
Responsible for planning, controlling, and implementing processes related to materials and finished goods storage and movement including: Materials Movement & Storage; Finished Goods Movement & Storage; General Logistics Optimization, and Planning & Control.
Procurement
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for work associated with obtaining goods and services for all types of business processes applicable to the organization including Indirect Operations (e.g., Office Supplies, Computers, Travel, Maintenance, Machine Parts, etc.); Direct Operations (e.g., Raw Materials and Services for Manufacturing, Production or Construction; Finished Goods for Retail, etc.). Activities include product/service sourcing, supplier selection, pricing/terms negotiation, order processing, contract administration, and supplier performance management.
Production Planning & Control
Positions in this sub-family are responsible for managing production to optimize the use of resources and meet production schedules and standards. Activities include developing and implementing production schedules to ensure the effective use of labor, tools, plant and equipment and to coordinate production output with inventory requirements; estimating resource requirements; analyzing production and operational data; and matching the organization's resources with demand to maximize output and minimize production costs.
