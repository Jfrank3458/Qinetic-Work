Communities of Practice

The Communities of Practice (CoPs) are groups of employees with similar skill sets, interests, and common workflows that aim to assist with problem solving and knowledge sharing throughout QinetiQ US. By connecting colleagues across all business units and teams, CoPs serve as forums for meeting with peers, engaging in meaningful discussions, asking questions, sharing solutions, and collaborating.

Each CoP is organized around a different field or capability and is moderated by recognized peer QinetiQ US experts. The Communities also set their own membership criteria and conversation topics, ensuring the group conversations are relevant and engaging.

If you have any feedback, including ideas for new CoPs, please reach out to the Community Moderators, listed under the "Cadence" section of this page.

Communities of Practice are available to all QinetiQ employees.

Communities of Practice are ongoing.

QinetiQ Toastmasters Club

Toastmasters International is a nonprofit educational organization that teaches public speaking and leadership skills through a worldwide network of clubs. Headquartered in Englewood, CO., the organization's membership exceeds 300,000 in more than 15,800 clubs in 149 countries. Since 1924, Toastmasters International has helped people from diverse backgrounds become more confident speakers, communicators, and leaders

General club details

  • Toastmasters is a well-regarded organization around the world, with benefits including leadership roles, speech crafting and delivery, an individualized learning program, and a supportive environment
  • Meetings primarily are composed of:
    • Multiple speeches/presentations by different members
    • Evaluation of speeches by an Evaluator
    • Free-form conversations, called “table topics”, focused on engaging other members present at the meeting
  • The club will run year-round with rolling admission to become a member.

QinetiQ US Toastmasters is available to all QinetiQ employees.
  • Meeting format will be in-person and virtual
  • Meetings are conducted monthly (2nd Wednesday)
  • Meeting start time usually 5:30pm
  • Meetings typically last 1 - 1.5 hours

QinetiQ Mentorship Program

The QinetiQ Mentorship Program (QMP) aims to build more well-rounded professionals by providing a mentoring relationship and development program that delivers relevant content, helps you to build your network, and introduces you to thinking more strategically. If you are interested in an opportunity to participate in professional development activities, gain exposure to company-wide initiatives, and be assigned a professional mentor, then the QinetiQ Mentorship Program is for you. The program's guiding principle is “learning, developing, and growing together.” Its ongoing, collaborative learning will facilitate growth at the highest levels of professional practice.

The QinetiQ Mentorship Program is available to:

  • Mentees: All staff
  • Mentors: Must have at least three years work experience; at least one full year with QinetiQ

The QinetiQ Mentorship Program (QMP) is a six-month long program that occurs twice per year, with applications in the Fall and Spring. Sessions and targeted discussions between mentor and mentee take place throughout each month.

Leadership Exploration and Development

The LEAD program spends the academic year developing and mentoring those identified as rising leaders across QinetiQ. The selected employees have demonstrated both high performance and notable leadership potential. After program completion, LEAD Fellows can be deployed into roles with greater responsibility, scope, and complexity.

LEAD Fellows:

  • Motivate and inspire others
  • Are self-aware, mature and resilient
  • Are accountable and open to feedback
  • Lean forward and take initiative with little direction
  • Cultivate a growth mindset and learning interest
  • Possess a positive, winning mentality
  • Are outcome driven, prioritizes and optimizes situations to achieve results
  • Think conceptually, connects the dots across the organization to solve problems
  • Communicate boldly with strong social and interpersonal skills

Applicants are invited to apply for the Fellowship. Requirements include:

  • 1 full year with QinetiQ
  • Mid-level position
  • Consulting, Operations or Data + Tech (select nominees) Career Path
  • Opt-In to the ITM Lifecycle with Completed Performance Review

The LEAD program takes palace every year from fall - summer. Sessions take place the 1st and 3rd Thursday of each month.

POC: Tim Horn

Career Champion Training & Development

Research shows that 70% of employees feel they do not get enough time to discuss their goals and career aspirations with their leaders. That is not unique to QinetiQ; that is an industry wide challenge. The Career Champion role is critical to truly taking care of our employees and ensuring we are creating and cultivating a people-first culture. According to Forbes,"People leaders tend to be more influential in motivating employees to do their best work and feel engaged."

Note: Only those who Opt-In to the ITM Lifecycle will be assigned a Career Champion resource.

In a fast-growing and busy company like QinetiQ, we need to carve out the resources required to make this happen. In this case, the people resources. We have amazing managers doing truly impressive things for our clients and our company. But some of them just don't have the bandwidth to focus on individual careers and the professional growth of every one of their people. Career Champions will be dedicated to this effort; listening to those goals and aspirations will be their number one priority. The research is clear: employees who feel like their voice is being heard are more likely to stay and be more productive.

We couldn't be more excited about the QinetiQ Career Champion effort!

Who should consider being a Career Champion?

  • PMs, DPMs, and TL that are currently handling the Career Champion function
  • Anyone else who loves to see people grow and develop!
    • Sr Consultant/Sr Analyst and above

Career Champion Training & Development takes place in a blended form. Invitees will receive an outlook invitation to participate.

POC: Email the Career Champion inbox

Association Sponsorships

The American Council for Technology-Industry Advisory Council (ACT-IAC) is an organization that encourages collaboration between government and industry professionals within the federal sphere. QinetiQ is a participating member of the ACT-IAC community. As a QinetiQ employee, you can take advantage of the following benefits:

  • Education and Professional Development: Acquire knowledge and skills to enhance professional capabilities and competencies. Learn how to lead and manage in a consensus-based environment.
  • Knowledge and Innovation: Stay up-to-date on the latest technologies, government programs and strategies. Acquire knowledge and innovative ideas that will enhance mission performance and lead to organizational success.
  • Seat at the Table: Work in partnership with decision makers in government and industry to shape thinking, strategies and solutions that will benefit government, industry and citizens.
  • Recognition: Receive recognition for excellence in program and mission accomplishment. Stand out in the community for leadership at the individual and organizational level.

If you'd like to start taking advantage of this free resource, visit the website and follow the prompts to create an account. Make sure to use your company email address when registering. ACT-IAC frequently sends communications with information on updates within the industry, access to reduced cost/free trainings, and networking opportunities.

In addition to the free resources above, ACT-IAC offers a variety of prestigious leadership development programs, for all levels. As part of our commitment to our employees, QinetiQ sponsors one candidate for each program per year to participate in the Associates, Voyagers, Partners, and GLOW program.
